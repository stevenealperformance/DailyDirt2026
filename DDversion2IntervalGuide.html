Training Guide

Weekly Structure & Workout Menu

A practical toolkit for riders — pick sessions around Wednesday and Thursday group rides, and around how you're actually feeling.

01 / Philosophy

The Idea

Group sessions on Wednesdays and Thursdays are primarily DH-focused with some riding mixed in. Those two days are your anchor. Everything else in the week should make you stronger for those sessions without showing up cooked.

Rather than prescribing a rigid plan, this guide gives you a toolkit. Pick workouts based on how you feel each week. Consistency over time matters far more than any single hard session. If you're fresh, go hard. If you're flat, go easy — or rest. Both are training.

02 / Context

What DH Actually Demands

DH runs are typically 2–5 minutes of all-out effort. Between runs you recover, then repeat. That means the systems we care about most are:

  • Anaerobic capacity — the ability to ride hard for 30 seconds to a few minutes and clear the resulting lactate.
  • Neuromuscular power — short, maximal bursts for starts, pumps, and out-of-corner sprints.
  • VO2 max — your aerobic ceiling. A bigger engine means faster recovery between runs.
  • Aerobic base — the foundation under everything. Built with easy miles, not hard ones.
03 / Toolkit

Types of Intervals

Each card below is one zone: what it is, how to do it, why it matters. RPE is on a 1–10 scale. HR percentages refer to your max heart rate — if you don't know yours, use the highest number you've seen on a hard effort as a working estimate.

Zone 02

Endurance

45 minutes – 2+ hours
How
Easy and conversational BUT steady — try to pedal as much as you can. A really easy long climb or flat terrain works best.
Why
The foundation. Zone 2 improves fat oxidation, mitochondrial density, and recovery. Don't skip this. The better your base, the more quality work you can absorb.
RPE
3–4/10.
HR
65–75% of max heart rate. You should be able to talk, but need to focus a little to keep pedalling.
Zone 03

Tempo

20–60 minutes
How
Moderately hard, sustained effort. Continuous, no intervals needed.
Why
Builds aerobic capacity and fatigue resistance. Low cost, solid return.
RPE
5–6/10.
HR
75–84% of max heart rate. Similar feel to sweet spot, but at a pace you can hold for 20, 30, 40, 60 minutes — up to 2 hours once you progress.
Zone 04

Sweet Spot

10–30 minutes
How
Slightly below threshold. Examples: 3 × 15 min with 5 min easy, or 2 × 25 min with 8 min easy.
Why
A lot of aerobic benefit for less recovery cost than threshold. Great for busy weeks.
RPE
6–7/10. You can speak in short sentences. These require focus, but you should be able to chat once in a while. Great for a super-steady climb up an FSR at a medium pace, then ripping down singletrack with some fatigue in you… repeat for a few hours if you have time. 🙂
HR
85–90% of max heart rate, steady breathing, consistent performance across intervals.
Zone 05

VO2 Max

3–5 minute blocks
How
A series of short intervals in blocks. Unlike the intervals above, you're trying to go as hard as you can each interval WHILE MAINTAINING YOUR FORM. So this should be challenging but very consistent across reps.
Why
Raises your aerobic ceiling. A bigger VO2 max means faster recovery between DH runs and more headroom above threshold.
RPE
8–9/10. These should require a lot of focus. Heart rate and breathing should build through the session, just like your perceived exertion. The ground you cover should be very close each time, but feel harder and harder.
HR
Should be climbing to 90–95% of max heart rate near the middle of each set, and you should be able to hold it there with consistent performance across reps.
Zone 06

Anaerobic Capacity

30 seconds – 2 minutes
How
Hard efforts with equal or double rest. Examples: 10 × 1 min hard / 2 min easy. Or 3 × 1 min hard / 2 min easy, then 5 min easy — repeat 3 times. These should make you fade a little, so NOT perfect pacing — try to start hard and hang on.
Why
The most DH-specific interval. It matches the duration and intensity of a race run and teaches your body to handle and clear lactate. Just because it's the most specific doesn't mean do it EVERY SINGLE TIME.
RPE
9/10. You're hanging on by the last 15 seconds of each rep. You should feel like you're losing a bit of performance, having to fight for these, and that they get harder every rep — even though you can't go as far.
HR
Don't chase a number here — all feel, all hard. HR lags the effort, so by the time it catches up the rep's over.
Zone 07

Neuromuscular Sprints

6–10 seconds · all out
How
6–10 seconds maximum effort, standing, in a big gear. 3–5 minutes of easy spinning between reps. Do 6–10 reps. Think of these like starting a race out of a gate.
Why
Peak power for start gates, accelerations, and punchy terrain. Short enough that heart rate stays moderate.
RPE
10/10 each rep. If you can repeat the same power on the last rep as the first, you nailed it. If you don't have power, gauge it by the distance you cover starting from the same spot each time. Mix up traction types across workouts, but keep it consistent within a single session.
04 / Structure

A Sample Week

The group rides are the non-negotiables. Build the rest around them.

Two quality interval days per week (Monday and Saturday) is the target. One is fine if the week has been heavy. Three is too many when Wednesday and Thursday are already loaded.

05 / Menu

Choose Your Own Adventure

Use this menu for your Monday and Saturday sessions. Check in honestly with how you feel before you pick.

Feeling Strong
"let's go"
  • Anaerobic: 10 × 1 min hard / 2 min easy
  • VO2: 5 × 3 min hard / 3 min easy
  • Race simulation: 4 × 3 min all-out / 5 min easy — or ride hard DH runs and see how close you can keep the times
Feeling Okay
"steady day"
  • Sweet spot: 3 × 15 min / 5 min easy
  • Tempo: 2 × 20 min continuous
Feeling Tired
"just move"
  • Zone 2 ride: 60–90 min easy
  • Openers: 20 min easy + 3 × 30s fast / 90s easy + 5 min cool-down
  • Rest (this is a valid choice, not a failure)
Feeling Fried
"shut it down"

Message Luke and Steve right away!

Rest. Eat. Sleep. Strength is built during recovery, not during work. A forced hard session on a bad day costs you more than it gives.

06 / Principles

Carry These Through

Hard days hard, easy days easy

Most riders' biggest mistake is doing everything at medium intensity. That builds fatigue without adaptation.

Warm up properly

10–30 minutes of easy spinning with a few short openers before any hard interval. Cold legs don't produce good efforts, and they're easier to injure. The longer you have to warm up, gradually build the pace to an endurance feeling, include the openers, then start. The intervals will always go better.

Log how you feel

A one-line note after each ride (legs, sleep, mood) tells you more over a month than any power number.

Progress, then recover

Every 3–4 weeks, take a lighter week. Cut interval volume roughly in half. You'll come back stronger.

07 / Sessions

The Workouts

Specific sessions for each zone. Rotate through them alongside the menu above. Warm up properly before every one. All prescriptions are work / rest unless noted.

Tempo Zone 03
Workout 01 · Ten-Minute Reps

Build the engine

6 × 10 min on / 2 min off — progress to 9 × 10 min on / 2 min off

Work at that sustainable tempo effort — moderate, steady, slightly uncomfortable but not straining. Start at 6 reps. Build to 9 reps over several weeks. The 2-minute rest is short on purpose — it keeps the aerobic system primed rather than letting you fully recover.

VO2 Max Zone 05
Workout 01 · Classic 30/30s

Our staple VO2 session

3 sets of (8 × 30s hard / 30s easy), 5 min easy between sets

Each "hard" is as hard as you can go while keeping your form and holding the same pace across all 24 reps. The 30s easy is active recovery — keep the legs spinning, don't coast.

Workout 02 · Descending Pyramid

Three ratios, hardest to easiest

8 × 40/20 → 8 × 30/30 → 8 × 20/40, 5 min easy between blocks

One block each at three work-to-rest ratios, hardest to easiest. Block 1 (40/20) is the toughest — most work, least rest. Block 2 (30/30) is the middle test. Block 3 (20/40) is where you find out how well you paced the first two.

Workout 03 · Full Pyramid

Descending reps, increasing rest

9 × 30/30 → 1 min rest → 8 × 25/35 → 2 min rest → 7 × 20/40 → 3 min rest → 6 × 15/45 — done

Fewer reps each block, with shorter hard efforts and longer easy ones — but the rest between blocks gets longer too. On paper each round looks easier than the last; your legs will tell you it isn't. By the final block, those 15-second efforts will feel much harder than they should.

Anaerobic Capacity Zone 06
Workout 01 · Attack and Reset

Focus on the final thirty

3 sets of (1 min on / 2 min off), 5 min easy between sets

Pretend each interval is 30 seconds long and try to survive the last 30. Keep your RPM higher than feels comfortable. Go as hard as you can — when your power starts to drop, shift to an easier gear and attack again.

Workout 02 · Committed Threes

Commit early, then hold the effort

7 × 3 min max / 5 min easy between

Start really hard for the first 60–90 seconds, then just try to hold on. By the end of each rep you should be working to maintain pace, not push it higher.

Neuromuscular Power Zone 07
Workout 01 · Gate Starts

Bust the cranks off the bike

8–10 × 8–10 seconds max / 3–5 min easy spinning between

From a near-standstill, standing, in a big gear, on a slight uphill so you don't need to shift mid-rep. Think of trying to bust the cranks off the bike.

Workout 02 · Repeat Sprints

Short bursts with a real reset between

4 sets of (8 × 5s max / 10s easy roll), 10 min super easy between sets

5 seconds of max sprint in a big gear, then 10 seconds of easy rolling in that same gear — don't shift. That's one rep. Do eight in a row for a set. Between sets, 10 minutes super easy: sit down, roll as slow as you can on the bike. The long rest is what keeps the power high for the next set.
